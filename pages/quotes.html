Quotations that I find profound

This is a list of quotations which I have found myself coming back to throughout my life, with footnotes where I have something to add. It will be under continuous construction.

See also: excerpts that I find profound

 

Science / Mathematics

  • "If we crave some cosmic purpose, then let us find ourselves a worthy one."[1] Carl Sagan, Pale Blue Dot
  • "If you wish to make an apple pie from scratch, you must first invent the universe." Carl Sagan, Cosmos
  • "Science is the great antidote to the poison of enthusiasm and superstition." Adam Smith, The Wealth of Nations
  • "There are no secrets about the world of nature. There are secrets about the thoughts and intentions of men." J. Robert Oppenheimer
  • "The first principle is that you must not fool yourself – and you are the easiest person to fool." Richard Feynman
  • "Our imagination is stretched to the utmost, not, as in fiction, to imagine things which are not really there, but just to comprehend those things which are there." Richard Feynman
  • "I mean the word proof not in the sense of the lawyers, who set two half proofs equal to a whole one, but in the sense of a mathematician, where ½ proof = 0, and it is demanded for proof that every doubt becomes impossible."[2] Carl Friedrich Gauss
  • "If I have seen further it is by standing on ye sholders of Giants." Isaac Newton
  • "Our experience hitherto justifies us in trusting that nature is the realization of the simplest that is mathematically conceivable." Albert Einstein

Philosophy / Lifestyle

  • "The life we endeavor to live should be better than the general practice, not contrary to it."[3] Seneca, Epistulae Morales ad Lucilium
  • "La lutte elle-même vers les sommets suffit à remplir un cœur d'homme; il faut imaginer Sisyphe heureux."[4] Albert Camus, Le Mythe de Sisyphe
  • "Og nu Livets uskyldige Glæder. Det maa man lade dem, de har kun een Feil, at de ere saa uskyldige."[5] Søren Kierkegaard, Enten-Eller
  • "Il est beau d’écrire ce qu’on pense; c’est le privilège de l’homme."[6] Voltaire, Candide
  • "Merece lo que sueñas."[7] Octavio Paz

Art / Music

  • “I haven't understood a bar of music in my life, but I have felt it." Igor Stravinsky
  • "Any great work of art … revives and readapts time and space, and the measure of its success is the extent to which it makes you an inhabitant of that world" Leonard Bernstein

Footnotes

[0] I have kept quotes in their native languages where possible. I generally try to read the native versions of texts that I find profound, though I will do so in alongside the English if I do not have a high enough level in the language (this is the case in French and Danish), and I will read the English text if I do not have any knowledge of the language.

[1] This has long been my favorite quote, taken from one of my favorite books by one of my favorite authors. It was my senior quote in high school and is still a quote that I live by to this day.

[2] This quote really gets at the essence of the beauty of the scientific mindset, especially that of mathematics. It captures why I have no interest in being a lawyer, but plenty of interest in working within science and mathematics.

[3] This translation comes from the 2015 University of Chicago press edition of this work.

[4] English translation: "The struggle itself toward the heights is enough to fill a man's heart. One must imagine Sisyphus happy." This quote, and broadly speaking, this work, changed my life when I first read it in high school.

[5] English translation: "And now the innocent joys of life. You have to hand it to them, they have only one fault: that they are so innocent."

[6] English translation: "It is noble to write as we think; it is the privilege of humanity."

[7] English translation: "Deserve what you dream."
